Professor's Rubric: What Earns Points ▼
Issue (20%)
Identify all distinct legal questions. State issues clearly as "Whether..." questions. Avoid stating conclusions.
Rule (30%)
State applicable law precisely. Include elements, tests, or standards. Cite the source (statute, case, doctrine). Explain multi-step rules fully.
Application (35%)
Apply facts to each element. Explain your reasoning. Address counterarguments. Show how rules map to specific facts. Distinguish unfavorable authority if needed.
Conclusion (15%)
State your final answer concisely. Should flow logically from your application. Avoid new arguments or unsupported claims.
